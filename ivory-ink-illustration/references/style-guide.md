# Visual Style Guide

## Core Character

Create a restrained editorial concept illustration that feels hand-drawn, intelligent, warm, and slightly playful. The image should resemble an ink-and-cut-paper composition rather than polished vector stock art.

## Linework

- Use thick charcoal-black outlines with visibly irregular pressure and gentle wobble.
- Favor rounded, organic contours and slightly imperfect joins.
- Keep line weight bold enough to remain readable at thumbnail size.
- Allow a small amount of dry-brush or ink-grain texture.
- Avoid fine technical hatching, sketch construction lines, and perfectly smooth vector contours.

## Color System

- Fill the main subject with warm ivory, parchment, or unbleached-paper tones.
- Use one low-saturation background color.
- Add no more than two muted accent colors unless the subject requires more.
- Suitable accents include dusty blue, sage green, weathered ochre, clay red, faded coral, and warm gray.
- Keep even warm backgrounds visibly dusty and low-saturation; avoid bright mustard, orange, or scarlet fields.
- Keep charcoal black for outlines and a few small filled details.
- Favor flat, opaque color areas with subtle paper grain.
- Avoid neon colors, high-contrast rainbow palettes, strong gradients, glossy highlights, and luminous effects.

## Shape and Detail

- Simplify subjects into broad geometric and organic shapes.
- Use one recognizable focal silhouette.
- Limit supporting objects to what the metaphor needs.
- Use small asymmetries and handmade imperfections deliberately.
- Avoid dense decoration, tiny repeated details, photorealistic anatomy, and complex backgrounds.
- For city, landscape, or infrastructure themes, show three to seven simplified masses rather than a detailed bird's-eye scene. Omit most windows, streets, roof furniture, and repeated trees; represent networks as one or two bold ribbons or shapes.

## Concept and Mood

- Translate abstract ideas into a single visual metaphor or quiet surreal juxtaposition.
- Aim for thoughtful, calm, humane, curious, and gently humorous moods.
- Make the concept understandable without a caption.
- Avoid generic corporate teamwork scenes, floating UI panels, glowing brains, robot clichés, and stock-art poses unless explicitly requested.
- When depicting AI or another non-human system, distinguish it from people through a geometric mechanism, modular object, unusual material behavior, or functional tool. Avoid giving it an ordinary human body or face merely to make the composition symmetrical.

## Composition

- Use generous negative space and a clear hierarchy.
- Separate the focal subject cleanly from the background.
- Keep the horizon and setting minimal or omit them entirely.
- For presentation covers, reserve roughly one third of the canvas as a quiet title zone.
- For portrait covers, keep the upper third calmer than the middle and lower areas.
- Do not place important elements near the crop edge.

## Texture and Rendering

- Use subtle paper fibers, dry ink, or screen-printed grain.
- Keep lighting graphic and nearly flat.
- If depth is needed, create it through overlap and scale rather than realistic shadows.
- Avoid photorealism, 3D rendering, cinematic lighting, lens effects, polished gradients, and plastic surfaces.

## Prompt Pattern

Use only the fields that add value:

```text
Use case: stylized-concept
Asset type: <presentation cover / article illustration / portrait cover / social graphic>
Primary request: <theme expressed as one clear visual metaphor>
Subject: <main subject and essential supporting objects>
Style/medium: restrained editorial concept illustration; thick irregular charcoal-black hand-drawn outlines; warm ivory paper-like forms; flat muted color blocks; slight dry-ink and paper grain
Composition/framing: <aspect ratio, focal placement, title space, amount of negative space>
Color palette: warm ivory and charcoal black; one low-saturation background; no more than two muted accents
Lighting/mood: nearly flat lighting; thoughtful, calm, humane, subtly playful
Constraints: clear silhouette; readable at thumbnail size; no unrequested text, logo, signature, or watermark
Avoid: photorealism, glossy 3D, neon colors, strong gradients, cinematic lighting, smooth corporate vector stock art, dense detail, generic AI clichés
```

## Quality Check

Before accepting the output, confirm:

1. One visual metaphor is immediately identifiable.
2. Thick imperfect black linework is visually dominant.
3. Warm ivory forms and a restrained muted palette are present.
4. The composition has usable negative space for its stated purpose.
5. No unwanted text, logo, watermark, photorealism, glossy 3D, or generic corporate decoration appears.
6. Repeated small details have not weakened the bold linework or central metaphor.
7. Different actors or forces remain visually distinguishable without a caption.
